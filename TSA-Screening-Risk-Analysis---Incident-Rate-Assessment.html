Question #5

  1. To help assist us with booking flights in the future, we want to create a function that identifies the time of day when an airport is on average the busiest. The goal is to create a single function where you can plug in an airports call letters and the output is the time of day when it is the busiest. Use your newly created function to report out the busiest time to checkin at the New York City area airports: Newark (EWR), John F Kennedy (JFK) and LaGuardia (LGA).
tsa_2022$checkins <- as.numeric(tsa_2022$checkins)
## Warning: NAs introduced by coercion
busiest_hour <- function(airport_code) {
  
  airport_data <- tsa_2022[tsa_2022$call_letters == airport_code, ]
  
  
  avg_by_hour <- aggregate(checkins ~ hour, data = airport_data, FUN = mean)
  
  
  busiest <- avg_by_hour[which.max(avg_by_hour$checkins), "hour"]
  
  return(busiest)
}

busiest_hour("EWR")  # Newark
## [1] "16:00"
busiest_hour("JFK")  # John F Kennedy
## [1] "17:00"
busiest_hour("LGA")  # LaGuardia
## [1] "09:00"

The busiest times to check in at the New York City area airports are: Newark (EWR): 16:00 or 4:00 p.m John F Kennedy (JFK): 17:00 or 5:00 p.m LaGuardia (LGA): 09:00 or 9:00 a.m

Question #7

  1. Let’s check which airport has the most guns confiscated relative to the number of people who actually go through the check points. To do this, aggregate both the TSA checkin data and the firearm data to get an annual measurement by airport. Merge the datasets together. If there were no confiscations at an airport, make sure this is recorded as a zero. Create a new confiscation rate variable. Out of the airports that confiscated more than 10 guns, which airport had the highest confiscation rate?
tsa_annual <- aggregate(checkins ~ call_letters,
  data = tsa_2022,sum)

names(tsa_annual) <- c("airport", "checkins")


guns_annual <- aggregate(date ~ airport,
  data = tsa_guns_2022,
  FUN = length)

names(guns_annual) <- c("airport", "firearms")


combined <- merge(tsa_annual,
  guns_annual,
  by = "airport",
  all.x = TRUE)

combined$firearms[is.na(combined$firearms)] <- 0

combined$confiscation_rate <- combined$firearms / combined$checkins

over_10 <- combined[combined$firearms > 10, ]

highest_rate_airport <- over_10[
  which.max(over_10$confiscation_rate),
]

highest_rate_airport
##     airport checkins firearms confiscation_rate

Out of the airports that confiscated more than 10 guns, Orlando International Airport (MCO) had the highest confiscation rate of 7.34.

The airports that stand out in 2022 for firearm confiscations relative to the number of travelers are ATL, DFW, LAX, and MIA. ATL had over 400 firearms confiscated with approximately 12,000 travelers, making it a clear outlier with a high confiscation rate. DFW also had a high number of confiscations, around 380, with roughly 22,000 passengers. On the other hand, LAX and MIA had fewer firearms confiscated, under 100 each, but a much higher number of travelers, around 23,000–24,000, which makes them notable for having a low confiscation rate relative to volume. Overall, these airports stand out because their confiscation numbers are disproportionately high or low compared to the general trend of firearms versus traveler counts at other airports.
